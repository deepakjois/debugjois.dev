#### Coffee Omakase
[Coffee omakase is Japan’s love letter to caffeine](https://archive.ph/22N5L#selection-251.0-254.0)

> Italy brought us cappuccinos. Australia introduced the [flat white](https://archive.ph/o/22N5L/https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/morning-mix/wp/2015/01/07/the-disputed-history-of-the-flat-white-the-coffee-drink-starbucks-just-introduced-in-north-america/). Cuba created the cafecito, and the Middle East, the qahwa.
>
> Japan is bringing us coffee omakase.

> In Japan, there are a number of cafes specializing in coffee omakase. Over the course of three days, I sampled four of them in a highly caffeinated journey through [Tokyo](https://archive.ph/o/22N5L/https://www.washingtonpost.com/travel/japan/tokyo-local-guide/) and Kyoto. It evoked the dining experience associated with high-end sushi, placing you in the hands of an expert to curate and overwhelm your senses. (Omakase translates to “I’ll leave it up to you.”)
