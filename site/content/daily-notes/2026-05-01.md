#### Maladaptive Frugality
[Maladaptive frugality - Herbert Lui](https://herbertlui.net/maladaptive-frugality/)

> When you default to the lowest cost option without considering the drawbacks, procrastinating or [hesitating on spending](https://herbertlui.net/waking-up-from-the-trance-of-hesitation/), or guilt tripping yourself about an essential expense or [making a recoverable mistake](https://herbertlui.net/making-mistakes-with-grace/), you’re engaging in maladaptive frugality. 
> 
> The most useful thing you can do is be mindful of it and try to [draw yourself into the present moment](https://herbertlui.net/meditating-through-self-forgiveness-frustration-and-overwhelm/). As Tim Ferriss [asks](https://tim.blog/2019/12/30/where-are-you-still-using-single-ply/), “What does your last year—not your childhood beliefs—tell you about where you might invest more for a higher quality of life?”
> 
> When you make frugality your servant, [it can offer you freedom](https://herbertlui.net/when-you-save-money-you-buy-freedom/). When you make frugality your master—maladaptive frugality—it traps you and limits your possibilities.
